About the Editor

In January 2015, I packed my bags and jumped on a flight to anywhere. Two flights and over 24 hours later I was in Bangkok, Thailand. After years of competitive schooling, universities, and jobs - my health began to fail at a very young age. I was in constant dental pain while trying to make a name for myself and navigate the complex and murky waters of business and relationships.

While my pain was great, it taught me to recognize the pain in others, opening my eyes to the suffering that each human being endures during our time here. After I returned to the U.S. from Thailand, I saw my old world with new eyes.

I struggled to reckon my old world of competitive business and technology, startups, advertising, research and science - with my new world of compassion, selflessness, care, and meditation. On some days, I felt swept away by the desire to create and sell products, to compete with information and execution. On other days, I felt an overwhelming desire for solitude and purity, wishing to cast away all worldly desires and live a monastic life.

Taking note from the Eastern spiritual practices, I decided to pursue the middle path. Opening the question, how can I have fun and play with technology and other human beings - while also not being swept up by the desires and fears that brought me so much suffering?

The Compassionate Technologies newsletter is my answer, a yearlong journey into balance. A playful, scientific, and sometimes edgy exploration of business and technology through the eyes of compassion.

You can find me online on Instagram, Facebook, or LinkedIn.  - Olivia Jeffers

Our Philosophy

We aim to cover and connect a broad range of fields in science, tech, and business. All pieces are short, fun, and to the point. We don't sensationalize and our capital is trust.

What Will Compassionate Technologies Cover?

Over the next year, we will link together the body, heart, and mind by reporting on the newest trends in research, technology, and business. June through September will focus on body technologies, the "here and now" - medicine, food, and water. October through January will focus on heart or emotional technologies, our connection to others - infrastructure, social media, and education. February through May will focus on mind and the future - artificial intelligence, brain computer interfacing, and space technology.

How Can I Subscribe?

The newsletters are released every Sunday morning, called Weeklies. Paid subscribers receive Weeklies directly to their doorstep or inbox two-weeks before they are published for free online. You can sign up here.

What are the Events?

As I have been writing and interviewing, I see that people are genuinely excited about these topics, but there isn't a platform or way to meet others with similar interests. With the income from paid subscriptions, I host local events - giving people a chance to get inspired and make new connections.

I love these, but can't afford to pay right now.

That's okay! Keep coming to the website or follow on Facebook or LinkedIn for weekly updates!

How can I help?

We are looking for writers, researcher assistants, and interviews. If you would like to be featured as a writer, assist in research, or can provide leads or interviews, please contact me at olivia@compassionate-technologies.org.
